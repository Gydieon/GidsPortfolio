Why Active Directory?

Active Directory is one of the most widely used identity and access management systems in enterprise IT environments. This project involved setting up and configuring a domain environment from scratch, managing users and groups, applying Group Policies, and simulating a real-world IT infrastructure with proper access control and security practices.

What I Learned

This project strengthened my understanding of how enterprise environments are structured and managed using Active Directory. I gained hands-on experience configuring a domain controller, managing users and groups, and organizing resources using Organizational Units to reflect real-world business structures. Working with Group Policy also helped me understand how administrators enforce security settings and control user environments at scale.

Additionally, I developed practical troubleshooting skills by resolving common issues such as login failures, account lockouts, and permission errors. This experience mirrors real IT support scenarios, where identifying the root cause—whether it is authentication, authorization, or policy-related—is critical to maintaining a secure and efficient network environment.
